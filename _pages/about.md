Hi! I'm Xinyi Qian, postdoctoral researcher jointly appointed by the Department of Geophysics (hosted by [Professor Paul Segall](https://cdfm.stanford.edu/people/paul-segall)) and the Department of Mechanical Engineering (hosted by [Professor Adrian Lew](https://me.stanford.edu/people/adrian-lew)) at Stanford University. I received my Ph.D. in Geomechanics and GeoSystems Engineering from the [Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering (GeoSystems)](https://geotechnical.berkeley.edu/) at the University of California, Berkeley. My Ph.D. was coadvised by [Professor Jonathan D. Bray](https://ce.berkeley.edu/people/faculty/bray) and [Professor F. Estefan T. Garcia](https://cee.engin.umich.edu/people/garcia-f-estefan-t/). Prior to joining Berkeley, I received my Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering from [Tsinghua University](https://www.tsinghua.edu.cn/en/) in 2018. My undergraduate thesis was advised by [Professor Xiaodong Ji](https://www.civil.tsinghua.edu.cn/ceen/info/1063/1217.htm).

Research Interest
======
My research interests are primarily in computational mechanics, particularly computational geomechanics. I am interested in both the theoretical formulation of numerical methods—from continuum-based to discrete approaches—and the simulation of material behavior and natural phenomena across scales, from microscopic to macroscopic. A central focus of my work is the analysis of the underlying physical mechanisms governing complex geosystems.
My current research focuses on finite element modeling of dike propagation with coupled solid–fluid interactions. Previously, my work involved high-performance discrete element simulations of granular materials. I am also familiar with the material point method (MPM). 
Beyond geomechanics, I have developed expertise in computer algorithms, scientific computing, and data analysis. I also actively integrate AI-assisted research workflows, including the development and use of custom AI agentskills to support scientific research.
